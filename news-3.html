How to choose good milk for the digestive system

Depending on your condition, disease, you can choose whole milk, low fat, yogurt to support weight gain or loss, supplement beneficial bacteria for the digestive system.

Milk is a rich source of protein, containing 9 essential amino acids that help the body function properly, including growth and development, cell repair, immune system regulation...

Currently, on the market there are many types of milk, adults and children should choose the type that is suitable for their nutritional status and disease.

How to choose the right milk

Whole milk

Milk provides calcium and vitamin D for strong bones and teeth, protein for energy and muscle, vitamin B12 for healthy red blood cells and nerve tissue, and niacin for metabolism. Milk has many important benefits for height growth in children and non-malnourished adults. Malnourished people should use milk containing a lot of energy and protein.

Low-fat or fat-free milk

Low-fat milk is high in protein but low in calories and fat and fat-soluble vitamins. Milk is suitable for overweight and obese people who need to lose weight or patients with high blood fats, especially high blood triglycerides.

Yogurt

Yogurt is made from milk through fermentation using friendly bacteria. According to Dr. Vu Thanh, yogurt provides vitamin D, calcium, zinc and potassium, which are often lacking in the daily diet. The beneficial intestinal bacteria in yogurt support the elimination of toxins, increasing the immune health of the digestive system. The body secretes less corticosteroids, reducing the amount of fat in the abdomen.

According to a review published in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, eating yogurt helps relieve constipation and diarrhea. People who are lactose intolerant from milk can use yogurt. The friendly bacteria in yogurt help regenerate bacteria in the intestines, for a healthy digestive system, improving food digestion time.

Note when using milk

As we age, the function of organs in the body such as digestion, joints ... declines. Many people experience poor digestion, making it difficult to absorb nutrients from everyday foods. According to Dr. Vu Thanh, the elderly often suffer from digestive disorders, weight loss, and anorexia, which seriously affect their health. Milk helps the elderly to easily absorb nutrients, supplementing intestinal microflora to improve digestive function. Using milk is a simple way to supplement nutrition and enhance the necessary nutrients for the body. This drink is rich in calcium and protein to help elderly people not need to eat too much and still be provided with enough energy, contributing to preventing osteoporosis and reducing muscle loss.

This is a complete food supplement for the body. Lactose intolerance is caused by the body not producing enough or not producing the enzyme lactase for digestion. This condition causes symptoms of gas, bloating, abdominal pain, diarrhea, nausea after drinking milk about 30 minutes to 2 hours.

Milk allergy is often confused with lactose intolerance. This is an atypical immune system response to milk and dairy products. Allergic reactions often occur after drinking milk. Symptoms can range from mild to severe including wheezing, vomiting, urticaria, gastrointestinal disturbances, diarrhea, severe anaphylaxis, respiratory constriction, flushing, shock, life-threatening hypotension.
